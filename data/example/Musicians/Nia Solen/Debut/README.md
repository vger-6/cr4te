Debut is Nia Solen’s first official album, released in 2015 when she was 25 years old. A confident and fully-formed introduction, the record announced Solen as a distinctive new voice in electronic pop. Blending warm melodic songwriting with crisp, forward-thinking production, Debut established the core aesthetic she would become known for: the sensual collision between nature and technology.

The album moves gracefully between bright, shimmering pop moments and more experimental textures. Songs like “Signal Bloom” and “Human Firmware” showcase Nia’s ability to craft emotionally direct melodies while embedding them in intricate sonic environments filled with glitchy light, pulsing synths, and organic field recordings. Thematically, Debut explores awakening — both digital and personal — as Nia reflects on identity, connection, and the strange beauty of a world increasingly mediated by screens and signals.

Visually, the album artwork and videos introduced her signature universe: overgrown gardens intertwined with glowing circuitry, soft sunlight breaking through digital interference, and lush botanical forms merging with futuristic design. Debut was praised for its freshness and cohesion, earning Nia early acclaim in the European electronic scene and building the foundation for her dedicated following.
