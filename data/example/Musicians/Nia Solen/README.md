Nia Solen is a Spanish electronic pop artist known for her distinctive ability to weave organic, natural imagery with sleek, futuristic sound design. Her music exists in a dreamlike space where nature and technology coexist — blending warm, melodic songwriting with intricate, experimental production.

Born in May 1990 in Barcelona, Nia grew up surrounded by the vibrant culture and Mediterranean light of her hometown. She began experimenting with music in her late teens, initially drawn to indie rock before fully immersing herself in electronic production. After studying sound design and visual arts, she quietly developed her project for several years. She made her official debut in 2015 at age 25 with the self-titled album Debut, a release that introduced her unique aesthetic to the world.

Solen’s work is characterized by a thoughtful contrast: bright, infectious energy paired with deeper, more textural experimentation. Her visual universe perfectly mirrors her sonic identity. Tracks often feel like living organisms pulsing with electricity, combining shimmering synths, delicate beats, and intimate vocals.

Her 2018 sophomore album Static Garden marked a significant artistic evolution and remains her most acclaimed project. The record explores themes of digital disconnection, emotional growth in a hyper-connected world, and finding stillness within chaos. Songs like the title track and fan favorites such as “Bloom Protocol” and “Light Leak” beautifully embody her signature style — organic yet synthetic, hopeful yet slightly haunted.

Now in her mid-thirties, Nia Solen has cultivated a dedicated international following through her immersive live shows and cohesive visual storytelling. She continues to push her sound forward, creating music that feels both emotionally resonant and sonically forward-thinking. With a growing reputation as one of Spain’s most original electronic voices, she remains committed to her core artistic tension: the beautiful collision between the natural and the artificial.
